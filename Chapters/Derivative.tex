\chapter{Derivative}
Consider the modulus function \(\abs[z]\) and define the \textit{norm}
\begin{equation*}
    \func{d}{z_1,z_2} = \abs[z_1 - z_2]
\end{equation*}
then the set of complex numbers over \(d\) creates a normed vector space. Suppose \(U \subset \Complex\) is an open subset and \(f : U \to \Complex\) is differentiable at point \(z_0 \in U\) if the limit 
\begin{equation*}
    \func{f'}{z_0} =\lim_{h \to 0} \dfrac{\func{f}{z_0 + h} - \func{f}{z_0}}{h}
\end{equation*}
exists.
